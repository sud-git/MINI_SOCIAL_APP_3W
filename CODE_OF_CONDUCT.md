# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for everyone in our community, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

### Examples of Behavior That Contributes to Creating a Positive Environment
- **Be Respectful** - Treat all participants with courtesy and respect
- **Be Inclusive** - Welcome and support people from all backgrounds
- **Be Constructive** - Provide thoughtful and helpful feedback
- **Be Professional** - Keep discussions on-topic and professional
- **Be Open-Minded** - Accept criticism gracefully and learn from it
- **Be Patient** - Help newcomers and contributors feel welcome
- **Give Credit** - Acknowledge and credit others' contributions

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics or identities
- Unwelcome sexual advances or attention
- Trolling, insulting, or intentionally disruptive comments
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Exclusionary language or behavior
- Threats or aggressive behavior
- Spam or off-topic content

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for:
- Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate corrective action for unacceptable behavior
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, and other contributions for violations
- Banning contributors who repeatedly violate the Code of Conduct

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Email communications
- Community events and meetings
- Social media when representing the project
- All spaces managed by the project community

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a Code of Conduct violation:

1. **Document the Incident**
   - Note what happened
   - When it occurred
   - Who was involved
   - Any relevant links or screenshots

2. **Report It**
   - Contact: [project maintainer email]
   - Or open a confidential issue (if available)
   - Include as much detail as possible

3. **Follow-Up**
   - Maintainers will acknowledge your report
   - Investigation will be conducted promptly
   - You'll receive updates on actions taken

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment** - Report is received within 48 hours
2. **Investigation** - Facts are gathered and verified
3. **Decision** - Appropriate action is determined
4. **Notification** - Parties are informed of outcome
5. **Resolution** - Corrective measures are implemented

### Possible Actions

Depending on severity:
- Warning and correction
- Temporary suspension from community
- Permanent ban from community
- Escalation to relevant authorities (if applicable)
